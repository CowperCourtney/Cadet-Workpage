What is This Page For?

Here is where I will post the modules for each weekly task, with assigned reading and discussion highlights.

Layered Approach to IUU Fishing

Illegal, unregulated and unreported fishing is a deeply complex problem. In this course, I want to build a foundation of not just providing knowledge of the subject matter but also reframing the way Cadets would brainstorm to apporach a problem.

Learning Outcomes

At the end of this course Cadets will have several industry relevant skills including data analysis training, research framework practice in a real life project, and the ability to work both as a team and independently to solve complex STEM related problems.

Below is a More Detailed Look at the Course Material

Fisheries Science

We have teamed up with the Florida Wildlife Commission Research Institute in St. Petersburg to assist in giving Cadets a hands on learning experience in the vast field of fisheries science. In order to understand the project from a variety of perspectives, Cadets will learn exactly what goes into managing species populations and ocean enviornments including an inside look at some of the amazing research projects that are currently underway at the institute. These include GIS mapping data projects, fisheries enrichment, data analytics and modeling, artificial intelligence tools, robotic monitoring device usage and law enforcement procedures for local IUU policing efforts. We hope to have in person meetings with several departments during our monthly meetups.

Data Analysis and Visualization

Cadets will have an introduction to basic open source analytical software so that they can build skills working with real world data and creating professional, protfolio worth visuals to convey their findings. These include programs such as Github, Brackets, RStudio, Tableau and more.

Final Project: IUU Museum Exhibit

The final goal for the Summer 2022 course is for Cadets to put their skills into action by creating an interactive IUU fishing exhibit for the American Victory Ship and Museum. Using a "Global to Local" layout, cadets will design the exhibit by adding their own statistical analysis visuals they created using current fisheries data as well as coding interactive elements to a real exhibit that will be displayed to the public in the museum.

A Few Things to Know Before Your Cadet Applies:

Age Restricted to 16+

There is no rank restriction, however due to the advanced material and the nature of the project we ask that only older cadets apply.

Essay Submission Required

This training will come at no cost to cadets, however due to the limited spaces we have for Cadets to participate we ask that you submit a one page essay explaining why you would like to attend the program (if the essay exceeds one page, that is acceptable)

This is a Unique Commitment

Unlike advanced trainings Cadets may be used to, this course is designed to be done remotely with in person meetings once a month and weekly independent assignments throughout the Summer. Please consider this before applying.
